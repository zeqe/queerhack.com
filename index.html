About

queer_hack is an upcoming hackathon for queer students being organized by students at UNC Chapel Hill.

What's a hackathon?

A hackathon is a weekend-long event where people gather to code projects, learn programming skills, network, and have fun! In a timed, highly collaborative environment, real problems are addressed in innovative ways. Hackathons are a great way for students to get a foothold into tech-related fields and gain experience.

Why queer?

Hackathons can be particularly intimidating and unwelcoming to queer students. Tech classrooms, hackathons, and companies have historically been dominated by cis straight white men. Queer people can feel isolated and excluded in these spaces, which leads to low representation and poor retention of queer students in computer science classrooms.

We are trying to change the culture of hackathons with the first queer hackathon - a hackathon hosted by and for queer students of all backgrounds.

Why do we care about queer in tech?

Our world is becoming increasingly defined by the devices in our pockets, our homes, and our workplaces. At the same time, technology is not free from bias; it can mirror and exacerbate the biases of the people who design it. Thus, it is vital that queer people and other marginalized groups be among those creating our digital world of tomorrow.

Our Purpose

We want to empower queer people to succeed in tech fields, build community, and inspire innovation.

Queer Empowerment

A hackathon provides students with an opportunity to build skills, network, and kickstart innovative projects. This is a space for queer students to jump into the tech field or further the journey they've already begun. Queer students should feel empowered to create and innovate here.

Building Community

As a queer student, it can be tough to feel at home in a typical tech space. We want to smash that feeling! (and the cis white heteropatriarchy!) Students shouldn't be turned away from tech fields simply because they are dominated by people who don't look like them. That's why we aim to build a community of queer entrepreneurs, programers, and code dabblers.

Inspiring Innovation

The programmers of our world design systems vital to our daily lives. We need queer programmers, leaders, project managers, and the like to design queer-friendly systems. Innovation starts with students and our team is working to create a place for that innovation to flourish at UNC.
